Timeline

Below is our current, actual, and planned timeline for this project.

Current status

This is the current status of the SRAM fingerprint project. Below is a list of updates from newest to oldest.

  • 2024.09.18: exploring strong 1's and 0's, maximum data retention time, and metrics for measuring a fingerprint.

Timeline of monthly milestones

Below are our planned milestones, in chronological order

August 2024

Have this milestone document and our project work plan documents initially written.

September 2024

Have all team members caught-up with previously collected data and the process for data analysis, and have a way to demonstrate that different chips are different.

  1. Run experiment: when is the max time for bits to stop flipping, and how consistent is that (the distribution) for a given chip. Such as the 0/1 bias percentage for individual bits.
  2. Come up with a process to identify chips with the Arduino, which is demonstratable/testable

October 2024

Have results for how the following can affect the fingerprints. And to see that if that affects the accuracy of our process for identifying chips.

  1. Adding a capacitor/inductor on the Vin line, and different voltage levels
  2. Chip temperature

November 2024

Have a usable metric that works as chip DNA, and a majority of progress in the presentation.

December 2024

Have a great presentation

Deliverables

The main anticipated deliverable for our project is a process for distinguishing SRAMs (Fingerprint) and SRAM families (DNA).
